Bitcoin

The Original Decentralised Digital Currency

Bitcoin is the original decentralised digital currency, launched in 2009 and widely described as digital gold. It combines a fixed supply with censorship-resistant transferability, making it useful as a long-term store of value and a tool for cross-border payments.

Origin & History

"A purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash would allow online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without going through a financial institution."

‚Äî Satoshi Nakamoto, Bitcoin whitepaper (2008)

Bitcoin started as a concise technical proposal and quickly became a global experiment in money. In October 2008 an author using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto published the Bitcoin whitepaper, describing a "peer-to-peer electronic cash system" that does not rely on a trusted intermediary. The network launched in January 2009 with the mining of the genesis block; early adopters ran nodes, mined blocks and tested the protocol's resilience in real-world conditions.

What followed was a gradual shift from a niche research project to a new asset class. Key early milestones include the first documented commercial bitcoin purchase (the 2010 "Bitcoin Pizza" transaction), the growth of exchanges that created fiat on- and off-ramps, and repeated cycles of speculative booms and sharp corrections. Exchange failures, regulatory scrutiny and technical improvements all helped shape today's infrastructure. By the early 2020s Bitcoin had attracted institutional custody solutions, corporate treasuries and regulated investment products ‚Äî broadening liquidity and market access while keeping Satoshi's core design (limited supply and decentralised consensus) intact.
