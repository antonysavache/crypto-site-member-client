Step-by-Step Kraken Walkthrough

Before you start: Have a UK bank account in your name, a valid ID, and an authenticator app for 2FA. First-time users should complete setup and verification via our Kraken Setup Guide; verified users can proceed below.

Buying ETH with Bank Account (Faster Payments)

Best for: most UK buyers building a position over time with low fees.

1) Open the GBP deposit screen

In your account go Funding → Deposit → GBP and choose Faster Payments. Copy the beneficiary details and the exact reference shown.

Screenshot: Kraken "Deposit GBP — Faster Payments" screen with reference highlighted

2) Send the transfer

From your bank, send the amount via Faster Payments using that reference. Funds typically land within minutes, 24/7.

Screenshot: Kraken "Funding history" panel showing GBP deposit credited

3) Buy ETH

Quick route: Buy/Sell → Ethereum (ETH) → enter the amount in GBP → preview → Confirm.

Price-controlled route: open ETH/GBP on Pro, select Limit, set your price, and place the order.

Screenshot: ETH/GBP simple buy ticket with GBP input
Screenshot: Kraken Pro — ETH/GBP order ticket showing Market/Limit selector

4) Check your balance

Your ETH appears on the balances page immediately after execution.

Screenshot: Balances screen showing ETH position

Buying ETH with CHAPS (high-volume buyers)

Best for: large, time-sensitive deposits where same-day credit matters.

1) Get CHAPS instructions

Go Funding → Deposit → GBP → CHAPS. Note the beneficiary, account number and your unique reference.

Screenshot: Kraken "Deposit GBP — CHAPS" instructions screen

2) Send before your bank's cut-off

CHAPS is credited the same business day if sent before cut-off. Your bank may charge a flat fee; this is negligible on large transfers.

3) Place your ETH order

For sizeable buys, consider a Limit order on ETH/GBP to manage slippage, or split the order into tranches.

Screenshot: Kraken Pro — ETH/GBP order book + limit order preview

Buying ETH with Credit/Debit Card

Best for: instant, small purchases when speed > cost.

1) Choose card checkout

Go Buy Crypto → Ethereum (ETH) → Pay by Card. Card must be in your name and 3-D Secure enabled.

Screenshot: Card payment flow with ETH selected and GBP amount field

2) Confirm the order

Review the total including processing/spread, then confirm. ETH is credited instantly. For regular or larger buys, switch to Faster Payments to reduce costs.

Secure Storage & Withdrawals

Short-term on exchange (convenience): keep ETH in your account if you plan to trade or stake soon. Enable 2FA and a withdrawal whitelist; see our Security Tips.

Screenshot: Security settings showing 2FA + withdrawal whitelist toggled on

Long-term self-custody (best practice): withdraw to a hardware wallet (Ledger or Trezor).

Withdrawal checklist (do this exactly):

  1. Open your wallet app and copy your ETH receive address.
  2. In the exchange, go Funding → Withdraw → ETH, Add address, and label it clearly.
  3. Send a test (e.g., 0.01 ETH).
  4. Confirm on Etherscan that the test arrived at the correct address.
  5. Withdraw the remaining amount.

Staking Options on Kraken & Alternatives

Staking ETH lets you earn rewards (typically 3–5% APR) while supporting the Ethereum network. Kraken offers a simple, custodial staking option:

  1. Go to Earn → Staking in your account.
  2. Select Ethereum (ETH) and review the current APR.
  3. Confirm the amount to stake — rewards start automatically.
Screenshot: Kraken Earn dashboard with ETH selected
Screenshot: Confirmation modal showing APR and payout frequency

For advanced users, liquid staking tokens like stETH (Lido), cbETH (Coinbase), or rETH (Rocket Pool) allow using staked ETH in DeFi. Be aware of peg risks during high volatility.

Key Risks to Consider

  • Slashing risk: Validators can be penalised, though Kraken handles operations and covers infrastructure failures.
  • Unstaking delays: Withdrawals from staking may take several days depending on network conditions.
  • Counterparty risk: Custodial staking relies on Kraken's solvency and security.
  • Peg risk: Liquid staking tokens (stETH, rETH) can temporarily de-peg in stressed markets.

Taxes & Compliance (UK)

In the UK, ETH sales are subject to Capital Gains Tax (CGT), and staking rewards may be treated as income at the time of receipt. Always keep records in GBP of your buys, sells, and rewards.
