Step-by-Step Kraken Walkthrough

Before you start, have these ready: a UK bank account in your own name, a valid passport/UK driving licence, and ~10 minutes for initial setup (verification can take longer at peak times). Enable an authenticator app (not SMS) for 2FA.

Who this is for: most UK buyers. It's the lowest-friction, low-cost route for regular deposits.

Step 1 — Create your account & lock security

  1. Go to Kraken's official site and Create account.
  2. Confirm the email link, then enable 2FA (authenticator app).

Quick troubleshooting — Faster Payments

  • Deposit pending? Re-check the reference string; mismatches delay credit.
  • Sent from joint/business account? Name mismatch can trigger holds; use a personal account in your name.
  • Paid on weekend/late evening? Most FPS is 24/7, but some banks queue; allow extra time.

Payment Methods & Worked Fee Examples (GBP → ETH)

Kraken offers several ways for UK users to fund their accounts, each with its own cost and speed profile. Choosing the right payment method can save you money on fees — especially for larger buys.

Worked Example — £1,000 Buy via Faster Payments

  • Deposit £1,000 via Faster Payments → credited same day, no Kraken fee.
  • Execute market buy (taker fee ~0.40%) → trading cost ≈ £4.00.
  • Net ETH received ≈ £996 (ignoring price slippage).

By contrast, using a card for the same £1,000 might incur higher processing fees, reducing net ETH to ~£985–£990. For large transfers, CHAPS is ideal, as the flat bank fee becomes negligible on a £20k+ deposit.

Staking Options on Kraken & Alternatives

After buying ETH, many UK investors explore staking to earn passive rewards. Staking helps secure the Ethereum network and pays you yield, typically expressed as APR (annual percentage rate).

Kraken ETH Staking

Kraken offers on-platform ETH staking with a simple opt-in process:

  1. Go to Earn → Staking in your account.
  2. Select Ethereum (ETH) and review the live APR (typically ~3–5% in 2025).
  3. Confirm and stake the desired amount.
Screenshot: Kraken "Earn" dashboard with ETH staking highlighted
Screenshot: Confirmation modal showing APR, rewards frequency, and terms

Rewards are distributed regularly, and you can track your earnings in the Earn dashboard. Note that unstaking can take several days depending on network conditions — Kraken handles validator operations and slashing protection for you.

Liquid Staking Alternatives

For advanced users, liquid staking protocols like stETH (Lido), cbETH (Coinbase), or rETH (Rocket Pool) allow you to stake and still have a token you can use in DeFi.

Screenshot: Diagram showing ETH → stETH swap + DeFi usage arrows

These tokens introduce smart contract risk and can trade at a discount to ETH during market stress — important to consider if you need instant liquidity.

Key Risks to Consider

  • Slashing Risk: Validators can be penalised for downtime or malicious behaviour (Kraken covers infrastructure but risk isn't zero).
  • Liquidity Risk: Unstaking isn't instant — check Kraken's current exit queue times.
  • Counterparty Risk: Custodial staking means relying on Kraken's solvency.
  • Peg Divergence: Liquid staking tokens can de-peg temporarily, affecting redemption value.

Withdrawals & Secure Storage

Buying ETH is only half the job — securing it is what protects your investment.

Option 1: Keep on Kraken (short-term)

If you plan to stake or trade frequently, you can leave ETH on the exchange — but enable all security features:

  • 2FA via authenticator app (not SMS)
  • Withdrawal confirmations via email
  • Withdrawal whitelist (only pre-approved addresses)
Screenshot: Kraken security settings with 2FA + whitelist highlighted

Option 2: Withdraw to Hardware Wallet (best for long-term)

Use Ledger or Trezor devices to store ETH offline. Checklist:

  1. Generate ETH receive address in Ledger Live or Trezor Suite.
  2. Add address in Kraken under Funding → Withdraw → ETH.
  3. Send a small test transaction first (e.g. 0.01 ETH).
  4. Verify on Etherscan that funds arrived at the correct address.
  5. Withdraw the full amount.

Risks, Compliance & Taxes (UK Focus)

Cryptocurrency in the UK is regulated as a cryptoasset, not legal tender. Kraken is FCA-registered, meaning it meets UK AML/KYC obligations. You must complete identity verification before funding your account — expect to provide photo ID and proof of address.

Tax Implications (HMRC)

ETH gains are typically subject to Capital Gains Tax (CGT). Keep records of purchase price (in GBP), date, and sale proceeds. If staking, rewards may be treated as income at the time of receipt — consult a tax professional.

DeFi and Staking Considerations

Yield farming, liquid staking, and DeFi protocols sit in regulatory grey areas. HMRC guidance is evolving — some rewards may be treated as income, others as capital gains.
