Mechanically, trading derivatives involves margin and maintenance requirements: traders post collateral, open leveraged positions, and must top up collateral if losses approach margin thresholds. Leverage settings and allowable multipliers vary by product and trading pair; always check the platform’s current limits before trading. For most users, derivatives are high-risk tools best suited to experienced traders or those who fully understand margin calls and liquidation risks.

Spot Trading and Margin

Spot trading is the simplest form — you buy or sell the underlying crypto and take custody of the asset. It’s the right choice for investors who want direct ownership or to hold long term. Margin trading lets a trader borrow funds to increase position size; it magnifies both gains and losses and requires careful risk management. Margin rules (available leverage, interest on borrowed funds, liquidation discipline) differ per market and by user verification level.
